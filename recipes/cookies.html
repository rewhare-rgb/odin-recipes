Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies Recipe

Discover the perfect balance of chewiness, gooeyness, and crunch in our Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies. With the rich nuttiness of the browned butter and the decadence of the chocolate chips, these cookies offer an elevated take on a classic favorite. Each bite delivers a delightful contrast of textures and flavors that's sure to make them your new go-to.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup (150g) dark/light brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cup (185g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 3/4 cup chopped dark/milk chocolate
  • Flaky sea salt

Steps

  1. Cook the unsalted butter in a saucepan until it foams and browns (5–8 minutes). Let it cool slightly.
  2. Whisk all-purpose flour and baking soda in a bowl.
  3. Combine light/dark brown sugar and granulated sugar with browned butter. Use an electric mixer to beat until incorporated.
  4. Mix in 1 egg and 1 tsp. vanilla extract on medium-high speed until the mixture lightens and thickens.
  5. Reduce mixer speed, add dry ingredients, and beat to combine. Mix in chopped chocolate by hand.
  6. Let dough sit for 30 minutes to hydrate; it will thicken.
  7. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C), line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  8. Portion dough into balls on the baking sheet, leaving space between. Don't flatten; cookies will spread.
  9. Bake until edges are golden brown and centers are soft (9–11 minutes).
  10. Let cookies cool on baking sheets for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
